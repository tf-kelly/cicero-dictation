Can we see hints of dictation in the language of Cicero’s letters?

Thomas Kelly · University of Cambridge

Writing from the Margins, Durham University · 15 May 2026

Overview Letter metadata Figure 1 Bibliography Materials Cite

Overview

This page accompanies a short talk asking whether dictation leaves a detectable linguistic signature in Cicero’s letters ad Atticum.

The handout, the letter `metadata' (evidence for individual letters being classified as dictated or autograph), and underlying data are all available below.

Letter metadata

The labelled corpus is derived from internal evidence within Cicero’s letters ad Atticum: explicit metalinguistic statements about handwriting or dictation, and inferences from context. Each letter ID links to the full text on the Scaife Viewer.

Table 1 · Autograph letters

  • Att. 1.1–2.22

    2.23.1: numquam ante arbitror te epistulam meam legisse nisi mea manu scriptam.

    Cicero states that 2.23 is the first letter not written in his own hand. The strong implication is that all — or at least the vast majority — of earlier letters were autographed. 1.10 is an apparent outlier based on its lingusitic profile.

  • Att. 8.2

    8.2.4: ego xiii Kalend., cum eadem lucerna hanc epistulam scripsissem qua inflammaram tuam.

    Cicero describes writing by lamplight, strongly implying autograph composition.

  • Att. 11.24 (second part)

    11.24.2: sed ad meam manum redeo.

    Cicero flags that he is returning to using his own hand, in order to discuss confidential material.

  • Att. 12.32

    haec ad te mea manu.

    Explicit statement of handwriting.

  • Att. 13.28.4

    hoc manu mea.

    Explicit statement of handwriting for the final section.

  • Att. 14.22

    certior a Pilia factus mitti ad te Idibus tabellarios statim hoc nescio quid exaravi.

    The verb exaravi (‘scratched out’) implies autography.

  • Att. 15.20

    hanc putavi mea manu scribendam, itaque feci.

    Explicit statement of handwriting.

Table 2 · Explicitly dictated letters

  • Att. 2.23

    numquam ante arbitror te epistulam meam legisse nisi mea manu scriptam … haec dictavi.

    Dictated while walking. Explicit contrast with previous autograph letters.

  • Att. 4.16

    occupationum mearum vel hoc signum erit quod epistula librari manu est.

    Explicit statement: letter in a scribe’s hand.

  • Att. 5.17

    hanc epistulam dictavi sedens in raeda.

    Explicitly dictated from a carriage.

  • Att. 7.13A

    si scriberem ipse, longior epistula fuisset, sed dictavi propter lippitudinem.

    Explicitly dictated due to eye trouble.

  • Att. 8.12

    dictare tamen hanc epistulam malui.

    Explicitly dictated due to eye trouble.

  • Att. 8.13

    lippitudinis meae signum tibi sit librari manus.

    Explicitly scribal hand.

  • Att. 10.3A

    alteram tibi eodem die hanc epistulam dictavi.

    Explicitly dictated.

  • Att. 14.21

    haec scripsi seu dictavi apposita secunda mensa apud Vestorium.

    Explicitly dictated over dinner.

  • Att. 16.15

    noli putare pigritia me facere quod non mea manu scribam.

    Explicit statement: not in Cicero’s own hand.

Table 3 · Dictated letters inferred from context

  • Att. 4.9

    eo die Neapoli apud Paetum. a. d. iiii Kal. Mai. iens in Pompeianum bene mane haec scripsi.

    Written while travelling.

  • Att. 4.10

    ad eum postridie mane vadebam cum haec scripsi.

    Written while travelling.

  • Att. 5.12

    plura scribam ad te cum constitero; nunc eram plane in medio mari.

    Written while at sea.

  • Att. 6.4

    haec festinans scripsi in itinere atque agmine.

    Written while travelling.

  • Att. 10.3

    10.3A contrasts this letter with one written mea manu the previous day.

    Inferred from the contrast in the subsequent letter.

  • Att. 11.24.1

    11.24.2: sed ad meam manum redeo.

    See autograph table (Table 1).

  • Att. 13.28.1–3

    13.28.4: hoc mea manu.

    The final section is flagged as written in Cicero’s own hand.

  • Att. 14.5

    haec scripsi ad te proficiscens Astura.

    Written while travelling.

  • Att. 14.6

    haec ad te scripsi apposita secunda mensa.

    Written during dinner.

  • Att. 14.12

    haec conscripsi x Kal., accubans apud Vestorium.

    Written while reclining at dinner.

  • Att. 14.16

    conscendens ab hortis Cluvianis in phaselum epicopum has dedi litteras.

    Written while boarding a boat.

  • Att. 16.1

    postridie iens ad Brutum in Nesidem haec scripsi.

    Written while travelling.

  • Att. 16.7

    haec scripsi navigans cum Pompeianum accederem.

    Written while at sea.

Beyond these labelled groups, a further 368 letters are unclassified. They form the rest category in Figure 1 below — a pool against which the auto / dict signature is tested.
